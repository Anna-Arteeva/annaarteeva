Principle 1: Don’t dive too deep too fast

The next step after sketches would be building inaccurate grey blocks in Sketch, and then.. switching to the next part. So I would constantly jump from one screen to another, verify all flows again, make a bit more research, talk to some more people, but not stay with one part until it perfect while everything else is empty. Working this way has lots of benefits, you do not waste too much time on perfecting things that will prove to be wrong in the end. And when you discover that the wrong solution, you do not try to advocate for them just because it cost you too much time to make it perfect. I don’t really bother myself with pixel-perfect spacing and super consistent typography until very very late in the process.
